01

The Brief

Yasha Taginya runs Wheaton Valet Cleaners and three sister locations across the DC metro. The existing Lovable.dev site loaded slowly, lacked a path for individual brand pages, and was never going to ship the inbound the team wanted before their fifth location — a federal site inside the Department of Education's Liberty Press facility — went live.

Scope: a complete rebuild on a stack the business could grow into, five distinct brand experiences under a single deployment, real intake (email + SMS), and an honest SEO foundation that didn't rely on tricks. Delivered as a fixed-price engagement under Hummus Development LLC.

03

Stack & Architecture

  • Next.js 16 + React 19 + Tailwind — App Router, server components by default, streaming where it earns its keep.
  • Framer Motion for entrance animations on hero, parallax on Liberty Press, and an accordion that doesn't fight the page.
  • Vercel deployment with cleanUrls, SSL via the platform, automatic preview branches per PR.
  • Resend for transactional intake email, domain-verified, RESEND_API_KEY set via printf not echo (the trailing-newline gotcha that breaks generated URLs is real).
  • Twilio SMS as a fallback intake channel; both wires drop into a single /api/intake endpoint that returns {"ok":true,"email":true,"sms":true}.
  • AI chatbot handling price quotes, hours, address-intent shortcuts, and Liberty Press routing — smoke-tested at 14 of 14 critical flows on the production deploy.
04

SEO & Trust

Every brand page ships with its own structured data so Google has unambiguous machine-readable signals — not generic boilerplate. Twelve JSON-LD schemas across the property: LocalBusiness, Organization, Service (per service), FAQPage, BreadcrumbList, plus location-specific PostalAddress.

  • A-grade audit on the standalone Vercel preview before DNS flip — meta, OG, canonicals, sitemap, robots, JSON-LD all green.
  • Audit verification rule: third-party SEO audits hit cached HTML half the time. Every claim was verified against a live curl | grep before action — saved hours of "fixing" things that weren't broken.
  • Real reviews only. No invented testimonials, no fake star counts. Where social proof lives on the page, it's pulled from real Google Business profile data with citation.
